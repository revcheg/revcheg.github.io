Dividend Adjustments on Cash Indices CFDs

CFDs on Indices will be subject to dividend adjustments. When a constituent member of an index pays Dividends to its Shareholders, dividend adjustments will be made to accounts of clients holding a position on the index.

  • Long Positions (Buy) – Clients holding long positions on the ex-div date will receive a dividend in the form of a cash adjustment (CD)
  • Short Positions (Sell) – Clients holding short positions on the ex-div date will be charged the dividend amount in the form of a cash adjustment (CW)

Calculated as Follows:
Dividend adjustment = Index Dividend declared x position size in Lots.

  • Long Positions (Buy) – Clients holding long positions on the ex-div date will receive a dividend in the form of a cash adjustment (CD)
  • Short Positions (Sell) – Clients holding short positions on the ex-div date will be charged the dividend amount in the form of a cash adjustment (CW)

Calculated as Follows:
Dividend adjustment = Stock Dividend declared x position size in Lots.
